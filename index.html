The Senses Architect

I’m an architectural designer, researcher and advocate for multisensory design, dedicated to creating Sensible spaces that enhance neuroinclusion, mental health, andwellbeing. I challenge the modern epidemic of "nonsense places"— environments that prioritize sight while neglecting the rich tapestry of sensory experiences that shape human connection to space. My work explores how places should smell, taste, sound, and feel to foster holistic, immersive, and inclusive urban living.
